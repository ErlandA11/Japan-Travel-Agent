Mount Fuji

Fuji is an iconic symbol of Japan and is located about 100 kilometers southwest of Tokyo. It is the country's highest mountain, standing at 3,776 meters (12,389 feet). Mount Fuji is known for its perfect volcanic cone shape and is considered a sacred mountain in Japanese culture. It has been depicted in numerous artworks, literature, and photographs and is a popular destination for tourists and climbers. Every year, thousands of people climb Mount Fuji during the summer months to witness the breathtaking sunrise from its summit.


Shibuya Crossing in Tokyo

Shibuya Crossing is one of the busiest and most famous pedestrian intersections in the world. Located in Tokyo's vibrant Shibuya district, it is a bustling hub known for its electrifying atmosphere and sheer volume of people. When the traffic lights turn red, all pedestrian signals turn green simultaneously, creating a mesmerizing spectacle as thousands of people cross the intersection from all directions.


Japanese Tea Tradition

The traditional Japanese tea ceremony, known as "sado" or "chanoyu," is a highly ritualistic practice centered around the preparation and serving of matcha, a powdered green tea. This centuries-old ceremony emphasizes harmony, respect, and tranquility. Participants wear traditional kimono and follow a precise set of rituals and gestures while enjoying the tea.


Ramen Noodle

an iconic and beloved dish in Japan. It has gained international popularity and is a must-try culinary experience for visitors. Ramen is a noodle soup dish that typically consists of wheat noodles served in a flavorful broth, topped with various ingredients such as sliced pork, green onions, soft-boiled eggs, seaweed, and more.
